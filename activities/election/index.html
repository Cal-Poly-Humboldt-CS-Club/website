FAQ

What is the time commitment like?

It's flexible ‚Äî you get to help shape the club! The time commitment depends on:

  • How often you hold meetings (weekly, biweekly, monthly)
  • The types of events you run (e.g., Coding Caf√©, workshops, guest speakers)
  • How well you delegate and collaborate with your officer team
  • Whether you organize extra events (like the Hackathon for Social Good or ICPC competition)

On the low end, expect an average of about 10 hours/week, especially if you're in a lead role. You can adjust based on your availability and team support.

Do I need to be a Computer Science major?

Nope! Officers can be from any major ‚Äî including Software Engineering, Math, Environmental Science, etc. As long as you're interested in tech, coding, or community-building, you're welcome!

Do I need advanced CS or leadership knowledge?

Not at all. You don't need to be an expert or a seasoned leader.

What matters most is:

  • A willingness to learn
  • Comfort with leading meetings (or growing into that)
  • Resourcefulness ‚Äî for example, inviting guest speakers on topics you're not familiar with

You're not expected to know everything! The goal is to build community, share learning opportunities, and supplement everyone's education ‚Äî including your own.

Are there any eligibility requirements?

No prior experience is required, but some campus rules apply:

  • The President and Treasurer must:
    • Have at least a 2.0 GPA
    • Be enrolled in 6+ units (undergrads) or 3+ units (grads with 3.0+ GPA)
    • Be in good standing with the university
    • Not be on any kind of academic or disciplinary probation
    • Undergrads must have fewer than 150 completed units, grads fewer than 50

Who is the club's faculty advisor?

Currently, it's Professor Ben Kovitz.

  • Each leadership team has the ability to change their faculty advisor.
  • Officers (especially the President and Treasurer) will work with the advisor regularly for things like fundraising forms.
  • The advisor doesn't need to attend meetings, but they should be kept in the loop.

Can I run for more than one position?

Yes! You can absolutely express interest in multiple roles. If you're elected to more than one, we'll ask for your preferred position, and the runner-up will be offered the other.
